arts and crafts

I've always enjoyed art and making things with my hands. From drawing to origami to fiber arts, I've explored a variety of mediums and love dabbling in different art forms. I find that I gravitate towards physical mediums where I can feel and hold what I'm making in my hands as opposed to mediums like painting or drawing. Art is one of my biggest hobbies and I spend a lot of my free time making things for myself and for people close to me.

origami

My first art hobby was origami, particularly modular origami, which is essentially folding multiple pieces of paper and putting them together into one structure. I especially liked making kusudamas, which uses multiple identical units to create decorative polyhedral shapes. For my senior project in high school, I created modular origami Platonic solids and Archimedean solids. This month-long project combined my passion for origami and math, and I found myself having to make geometric calculations to design the modular units and plan how they would fit together. Origami, and paper crafts in general, is still something I really enjoy making.

Much of my breaks since then were spent crocheting various things - mainly plushies and little figurines but also coasters and washcloths. Lately I've also been making tapestry and flowers. music

Music has been a big part of my life since a very young age. I started by playing piano when I was 5 years old, then started playing clarinet in 4th grade, then took vocal lessons in middle school, and eventually clarinet was the instrument that stuck. I've always played in school ensembles, including concert bands in middle and high school and orchestra in college, and I hope to find an ensemble to play in wherever I go in the future.
